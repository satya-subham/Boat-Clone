Finding the right design for your ears

A. In-ear Design

You might know them by various names like earbuds, in-ear headphones, these are the smallest and most portable type of earphones. They rest in the bowl of the ear, outside the ear canal, though a portion might extend into the canal. They have foam or rubber tips to give you comfort.

Some models, particularly sports centric ones, come equipped with wings or fins for a tailored and secure fit. These insert-style models when inserted into the canals often form a seal that can help keep out the extraneous noise. They are extremely popular these days due to their stylish looks and convenience.

Talking about the benefits of this design - the first thing that comes to the mind is their small size and hence the portability they offer. You can stuff them in your pockets or carry them in your palm. They don’t mess up your hair or interfere with your glasses or earrings! You can carry them effortlessly and always be at your stylish best!

B. Ear Clip Design

If you are looking for a middle ground between huge and bulky headphones and extremely compact and portable earbuds, then ear-clip design earphones are meant for you. In a lot of ways they carry the best of both.

These headphones are compact and have ear-loops to keep them in place. Mind you they are not like having ear tips, but a complete feature hook that fits over the ears and connects to your music device via Bluetooth or audio jack. This makes them easier to carry around than heavy headphones and more comfortable than earbuds.

On-ear clip-on headphones bestow protection to the user from different kinds of gory bacteria that might enter the ear canal and lead to infections.

C. Behind the Neck Design

We are sure you have already seen people wearing these high-tech collars with headphones sprouting out the sides. They go around with different names like - neckband headphones, collar headphones or even behind the neck headphones.

This distinctive style of design offers them multiple advantages. The first major advantage is quite an obvious one, but still worth pointing out - they are always around whenever you want them. Given that the earbuds are hanging around your neck, mere inches away from your ears, you won’t have to dig through your bag or pockets when you want to listen to your favorite tune or take a call.

Apart from this, they also come with many surprises like convenient controls, better mics that are nearer to the mouth, and bigger batteries. In the end, they say ‘I am on the go’ or ‘I am active’ and add that extra edge to your personality - Neckbands vs TWS: What Do You Need?

Features you should look out for

There’s a lot of jargon that goes around when talking about earphones. You might have already heard them in random conversations every now and then.

Read on to understand them better as you definitely need to have fair understanding of these in order to make the right choice:

  • Sound Quality & Drivers

    • Audiophile or not - we are sure that the quality of sound is a concern for all when considering buying the perfect earphones. Be it music, podcasts or movies - you want a pair that does justice to every note and ensures that you get to listen to your music the way it’s meant to be!

    Drivers are one such core component of any headphone that are instrumental in ensuring good sound quality. They boost the bass, mids and the treble to allow for an enjoyable listening experience. They basically convert the electrical signal feed to the headphones into an audible sound signal that can be perceived by the human ear. There are various types of drivers that are used in earphones, including the most commonly used moving coil (dynamic) drivers, balanced armature drivers, planar magnetic drivers and electrostatic drivers, among others. They use different technologies to power the sound, with some technologies offering better, more powerful sound than others. They also provide better isolation due to the absence of a vent within.

    In most cases, the larger the driver, the better the overall sound quality. For example, boAt’s Airdopes 381 come with 7mmX 2 drivers and pump out phenomenal music!

  • IPX rating

    • Let's just admit - we are a little careless when it comes to taking care of our earphones! They are vulnerable to everyday wear and tear - be it dust, splash of water - or even sweat - anything can cause them damage! For example - if you depend on music to keep you pumped up while you sweat it out at the gym or want to go out for a jog while it’s pleasantly drizzling outside then you would not want to enjoy it all without sweat or rain drops damaging them.

    That’s where IP rating comes into the picture!

    IP stands for Ingress Protection and also referred to as International Protection. IPX rating is the marking that denotes the level of protection against water, dust, etc. the IPX may also alternatively denote the object being ‘weather-resistant’, ‘waterproof’, or ‘dust resistant’

    The higher the IPX rating, the better protection is:

  • IPX4- Protects against splashing water
  • IPX5- Protects against water jets
  • IPX6- Protects against powerful water jets
  • IPX7- Protects in water up to 3 feet.
  • IPX8- Protects when immersed in water over 3 feet
